CUET 2025 Undergraduate Syllabus

A comprehensive guide for non-math students in Accountancy, Business Studies, Economics, English and the General Aptitude Test

1. Accountancy

The syllabus for Accountancy is divided into three parts:

Part 1: Accounting for Not-for-Profit Organizations and Partnership Firms

Unit I: Accounting Not-for-Profit Organization
  • Meaning, examples, fund-based vs. non-fund-based accounting.
  • Preparation of Income & Expenditure Account and Balance Sheet.
Unit II: Accounting for Partnership
  • Partnership deed, fixed vs. fluctuating capital, profit division.
Unit III: Reconstitution of Partnership
  • Changes in profit-sharing ratio, goodwill valuation, admission/retirement/death of a partner.
Unit IV: Dissolution of Partnership Firm
  • Meaning, settlement of accounts.

Part 2: Company Accounts and Financial Statement Analysis

Unit V: Accounting for Share and Debenture Capital
  • Share capital, debenture issue/redemption.
Unit VI: Analysis of Financial Statements
  • Tools for financial analysis, accounting ratios.
Unit VII: Statement of Changes in Financial Position
  • Cash flow statement preparation.

Part 3: Computerized Accounting System

Unit I: Overview of Computerized Accounting System
  • Concept, features, structure.
Unit II: Using Computerized Accounting System
  • Installation steps, data entry/validation.
Unit III: Accounting Using Database Management System (DBMS)
  • Tables, queries, forms, reports.
Unit IV: Applications of Electronic Spreadsheets
  • Depreciation schedules, payroll accounting.

2. Business Studies

The syllabus covers principles of management, business finance, marketing, and entrepreneurship.

Part 1: Principles and Functions of Management

Unit I: Nature and Significance of Management
  • Objectives, levels of management, functions (planning, organizing, staffing).
Unit II: Principles of Management
  • Fayol’s principles; Taylor’s scientific management.
Unit III: Business Environment
  • Dimensions (economic, social), impact of liberalization/globalization.
Unit IV: Planning
  • Features, process, types (objectives, strategy).
Unit V: Organizing
  • Steps in organizing; delegation vs. decentralization.
Unit VI: Staffing
  • Recruitment/selection process; training methods.
Unit VII: Directing
  • Motivation theories (Maslow), leadership qualities.
Unit VIII: Controlling
  • Steps in the control process.

Part 2: Business Finance and Marketing

Unit IX: Business Finance
  • Capital structure; fixed/working capital needs.
Unit X: Financial Markets
  • Money market vs. capital market; SEBI functions.
Unit XI: Marketing
  • Marketing mix; product life cycle.
Unit XII: Consumer Protection
  • Consumer rights; redressal mechanisms.
Unit XIII: Entrepreneurship Development
  • Characteristics, importance.

3. Economics

The syllabus focuses on microeconomics and macroeconomics concepts.

Microeconomics

  • Consumer Behavior and Demand: Utility analysis; demand elasticity.
  • Producer Behavior and Supply: Production function; cost concepts; revenue types.
  • Market Forms: Perfect competition; monopoly; monopolistic competition.

Macroeconomics

  • National Income: Concepts (GDP, GNP); methods of calculation.
  • Money and Banking: Functions of money; credit creation by banks.
  • Government Budget: Components (revenue/expenditure); fiscal deficit.
  • Balance of Payments: Current account vs. capital account.

4. English

The English syllabus tests reading comprehension, grammar proficiency, vocabulary skills, and verbal ability.

Key Topics

  • Reading Comprehension: Factual/narrative/literary passages (200–350 words).
  • Grammar: Tenses; active/passive voice; direct/indirect speech; clauses.
  • Vocabulary: Synonyms/antonyms; idioms/phrases; one-word substitutions.
  • Verbal Ability: Sentence rearrangement (parajumbles); choosing the correct word.

5. General Aptitude Test

This section evaluates reasoning abilities, general knowledge, numerical aptitude, and current affairs.

Key Topics

  • General Knowledge: Awards/honors; books/authors; Indian history/science/sports.
  • Current Affairs: National/international updates.
  • Logical Reasoning: Blood relations; coding-decoding; statement-conclusion reasoning.
  • Numerical Ability: Percentages; profit/loss; mensuration (2D/3D).
  • Quantitative Reasoning: Arithmetic series; data interpretation problems.
